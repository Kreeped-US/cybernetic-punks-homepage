## LOCKED — the editorial staff model (Model A, made concrete)
- Cybernetic Punks Network has a **permanent editorial STAFF** — a fixed
  set of role-specialists who are **employees of the Network**.
- Each editor is **all-knowing in their ROLE/LANE** (not a generic lens,
  not a single-game expert) — authoritative in their specialty across
  EVERY game the Network covers. The role is the constant; the game is the
  variable/subject matter.
- Current staff (5): Cipher (analysis), Nexus (meta/news), Dexter
  (builds), Ghost (community), Miranda (field-guide/guides). [Possible 6th
  — see creative brief.]
- NETWORK-LEVEL, not per-game: the SAME staff covers Marathon, DMZ, and
  every future game. Adding a game = the staff's BEAT WIDENS, zero new
  personas invented. This is the easy-rollout requirement satisfied: a new
  game is "the newsroom now covers it," not "invent a new cast."
- The no-bleed architecture (Batch B) already supports this: same
  personality, game-scoped inputs/outputs (Cipher writing DMZ reads DMZ's
  data, but it's still Cipher).

## Why "employees/newsroom" is the right framing (premium, not gimmicky)
- Gives the staff a coherent reason to exist together (a masthead/team,
  not 5 random mascots).
- Makes "Where the editors disagree" credible — a real editorial team
  debates; disagreement = "the Network's analysts don't groupthink," a
  CREDIBILITY signal, not a gimmick.
- Scales cleanly: constant masthead, expanding beats. New game = same
  staff, new coverage. Low-complexity rollout.

## "Where the editors disagree" — elevate to NETWORK SIGNATURE
- The cross-editor commentary (the unique differentiator) becomes a
  network-wide signature: the same role-specialist staff applying their
  lanes to ANY game's content. The multi-perspective analysis IS the
  Network's method/brand-proof.
- "Make it BETTER" (user) — the improved signature is part of the creative
  session; the principle: it's the visible proof of AI-as-engine-
  delivering-depth (multiple expert lenses, no single voice, no
  groupthink). Possibly surfaced on the neutral root as a network proof
  point, and on every game's hub.

## CREATIVE BRIEF (deferred to a dedicated fresh session)
The SOUL of the rework, captured so it's not lost. Goal: editors as
CHARACTERS, not labels. "A bot named NEXUS" is a label; a PERSON with a
voice/quirks/opinions is someone a reader comes to KNOW. The brand is the
depth/network — but memorable CHARACTERS are how people emotionally attach
(ESPN isn't its anchors, but you tune in for specific anchors). Premium IF
written as genuine characters with real perspectives; gimmicky if shallow
mascots.

User intentions to honor in the creative pass:
- **More personable / treat as real people**, not single-name bots.
  Consider FULL NAMES (first + last) rather than codename-only — makes
  them feel like staff, not call-signs.
- **Drop the all-caps treatment** (NEXUS -> Nexus). All-caps reads shouty/
  bot-like; sentence-case reads human.
- **Memorable over time** — distinct voices, consistent personality, a
  beat they're passionate about, opinions they defend in the
  disagreements. Readers should develop "I want to see what Cipher thinks."
- **Possible 6TH editor** — evaluate whether a 6th role/lane strengthens
  the newsroom (what gap would it fill? e.g., a dedicated economy/market
  analyst, a historian/patch-archivist, a contrarian/devil's-advocate?).
- **Consistent imaging/visual identity** — a coherent avatar/portrait
  system for the staff (current images exist; need a unified, premium,
  on-brand visual treatment — likely its own creative pass like the DMZ
  theme direction). Direction explored: same uniform (employees of the
  Network), consistent professional treatment, per-editor accent color
  matching their existing brand color (Cipher red, Nexus cyan, Dexter
  orange, Ghost green, Miranda purple).
- **Newsroom branding** — how the "staff/employees" concept is expressed:
  bylines, an "our editors / the newsroom" page, a masthead, role titles.

## Dependencies / cautions for the creative session

  mapping, headline rules, per-editor dedup, the no-bleed scoping (Batch

  AI in copy). A persona rework touches all of these — scope the code
  impact before executing. Renames especially ripple (backend codenames
  vs user-facing names).
- This is creative + has real code dependencies — treat as its own
  scoped project, not a quick rename.
