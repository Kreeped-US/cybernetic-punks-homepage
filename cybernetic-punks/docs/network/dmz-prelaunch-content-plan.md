# DMZ Pre-Launch Content Campaign — Plan

## Status
PLAN / strategy doc (not a build brief yet). Defines what pre-launch DMZ
content to make, why, and in what order. DMZ + MW4 launch Oct 23, 2026.

## Strategy (locked)
Do NOT compete in the pre-launch REVEAL-SUMMARY / hype race — the
established CoD sites (CODCentral et al.) have the authority, audience, and
staff to win it, and a new site auto-generating speculation from the same
public marketing both LOSES that race AND risks training Google to see the
DMZ section as thin AI content right before launch (hurting launch-day
ranking).

WIN the LAUNCH-DAY DATA RACE instead: nobody has real DMZ systems data
until the game ships; that queryable systems depth is what the architecture
is built for and a human-staffed blog can't maintain. Pre-launch content
should BUILD AUTHORITY + SEO POSITION THAT COMPOUNDS into that launch-day
advantage.

Unifying rule: every pre-launch DMZ piece must either (a) demonstrate real
cross-game authority we actually have, or (b) honestly scaffold a page that
flips to real data at launch. Neither requires faking knowledge of an
unreleased game — that's how we get pre-launch presence WITHOUT the
content-farm trap. Quality-over-volume applies here too (no long-tail
carpet-bomb of thin skeletons — Google penalizes that, possibly site-wide).

## Two tracks + production model

### Track A — Flagship authority pieces (DEFERRED behind editor rework)
- Few, higher-touch/curated, cross-game analysis that only a NETWORK brand
  can write ("what DMZ's design means for the extraction meta," "how
  Marathon and DMZ approach the same problem"). The brand's voice at its
  strongest; must be genuinely good (they're the proof of "premier
  intelligence").
- SEQUENCING DEPENDENCY (locked): these are written in the EDITORS' voices,
  and the editor persona rework comes FIRST (DMZ content should launch in
  the NEW voices). So flagship pieces WAIT for the editor rework — don't
  produce voiced DMZ content in the current personas then rewrite.

### Track B — SEO scaffolding (START NOW — voice-light, no editor dependency)
- The part that's safe to build before the editor rework (it's page
  structure + honest "tracking at launch" framing, not editor-voiced
  takes).
- LOCKED SCOPE: just the 3 confirmed system hubs that ALREADY have data-fed
  shells from Step 4 — 3D Printer, FOB, Hajin Regions. Scaffolding these =
  giving existing shells their honest pre-launch content; zero new
  architecture, zero sprawl. (Gunsmith/Loadouts + Missions/Extraction are
  also confirmed-real and can be ADDED LATER once these 3 prove the
  pattern; held for now.)
- Individual ENTITIES (specific weapons, recipes, map sub-regions, contract
  lists) WAIT for launch-day real data — building them now would be faking
  it, and holding them IS the launch-day-data-race play.

## Confirmed vs unconfirmed (sourcing, as of mid-June 2026)
CONFIRMED systems (solid to scaffold as honest hub pages):
- Hajin Exclusion Zone — the setting / map ("living, breathing combat
  environment").
- 3D Printer — converts scavenged loot/materials into equipment.
- FOB — base where the printer lives; progression hub; loot/cash/resources
  affect between-match prep.
- (Also confirmed but HELD: redesigned Gunsmith tied to in-match cash +
  progression; missions/contracts with rewards tied to successful
  extraction; dynamic weather/shifting objectives; solo + squad; ties to
  MW4 progression.)
TOO THIN to scaffold as entities yet: specific weapons, loadouts, recipes,
map sub-regions, economy numbers, contract lists — confirmed to EXIST, but
no public specifics. Category/hub page = honest; individual entity pages =
faking. Build entities at/after launch.

## What an honest scaffold page contains (the design principle)
Each of the 3 hub pages, pre-launch, honestly framed:
- A clear explanation of the SYSTEM as confirmed (what the 3D Printer / FOB
  / Hajin Zone IS, from confirmed sources) — real, useful, genuine
  authority content that can rank.
- Explicit "tracking at launch" framing — states plainly that
  recipes/regions/data go live when the game ships Oct 23. Honesty is both
  a quality signal and an SEO signal (Google rewards genuine, clearly-
  scoped pages over thin fakes).
- The STRUCTURE/skeleton ready to flip to real data on day one (the page
  exists, is indexed, accrues authority pre-launch; launch = fill it, not
  create it).
- NO faked/placeholder/speculative data presented as fact. No invented
  recipe lists, no made-up region names, no fabricated stats.
- Cross-links to the genuine cross-game authority angle where natural
  (extraction-shooter context only this network has).

## Sequencing
1. Track B SEO scaffolding NOW — the 3 confirmed hubs (Printer/FOB/Hajin),
   honest "tracking at launch" pages on the existing Step-4 shells. (Build
   brief = future; this doc is the plan.)
2. Editor persona rework (its own session) — must precede voiced DMZ
   content.
3. Track A flagship authority pieces — after the rework, in the new voices.
4. Launch day (Oct 23) — flip hubs to real data; build individual entity
   pages from real systems data (the data-race win); add the held hubs
   (Gunsmith/Loadouts, Missions) as real data arrives.
5. The 5 parameterization-pending 'marathon' sites flip to per-game when
   DMZ editorial actually starts producing.

## Notes / cautions
- Honest-scoping is non-negotiable (locked): real structure, no faked data.
  This is the brand quality bar applied to scaffolding.
- These scaffold pages are voice-light by design so they DON'T depend on
  the editor rework — that's why Track B can start first.
- Community/Reddit presence remains the repeatedly-flagged highest-value
  non-code growth lever, independent of this content plan — worth pairing
  with the campaign when DMZ content goes live.
- Verify confirmed-systems list against latest reveals before building (CoD
  marketing will add detail through summer/fall; re-check before launch).
